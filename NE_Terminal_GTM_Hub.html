Sales Playbook

Field reference for the Terminal & Transfer Systems Specialist. Read Sections 1–4 before your first prospect call.

What is a fuel terminal? Fuel terminals are the critical mid-to-downstream nodes in the fuel supply chain. Fuel arrives by pipeline, marine barge/tanker, or rail, is stored in above-ground tanks, and is loaded onto tank trucks for distribution to gas stations, airports, heating oil dealers, and industrial users.

The average US terminal has 19 storage tanks. The US has 1,296 transportation fuel terminals with 911 million barrels combined capacity. The Northeast is one of the densest terminal clusters in the country, driven by port access (Boston, Providence, Albany, New York Harbor, Philadelphia, South Portland) and the Colonial and Buckeye pipeline systems.

Key NE infrastructure: Colonial Pipeline (primary NE gasoline/diesel supply), Buckeye Pipeline (NE distribution), Laurel Pipeline (PA), Portland-Montreal Pipeline (ME/VT/QC). Marine import terminals handle international and Gulf Coast product via barge and tanker.
